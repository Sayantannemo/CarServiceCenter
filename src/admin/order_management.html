Order Management

1. Order Listing and Details

Display a list of all orders placed by customers, including details such as order ID, customer name, service requested, order status, date/time placed, and payment status.

Allow administrators to view/edit detailed information about each order, including service details, pricing, customer contact information, and any special instructions.
2. Order Processing

Provide tools to manage the order processing workflow, such as order approval, scheduling service appointments, assigning tasks to maintenance personnel, and tracking progress.

Update order status (e.g., pending, processing, completed, cancelled) based on the order's current stage.
3. Billing and Invoicing

Generate invoices for completed orders, including detailed breakdowns of services, parts used, labor costs, taxes, and total amount due.

Track payment statuses and manage billing records, including payment methods used (credit card, cash, etc.) and payment dates.
4. Communication and Notifications

Implement communication tools to send notifications to customers regarding order status updates, appointment reminders, payment confirmations, and service completion notices.

Allow for internal messaging or notes related to specific orders for internal communication among staff members.
